*A well placed sock to the back of the neck, and even the staunchest
guard will crumble like [goblin](/wiki/Goblins "wikilink") architecture. This
is the perfect time to [borrow](/wiki/Steal "wikilink")
[belongings](/wiki/Category%3A_Objects "wikilink") or
[gold](/wiki/Gold_In_Hand "wikilink") from lenders who might otherwise be
stingy. Several [Rogue guilds](/wiki/Rogue_Guild_Terrain "wikilink") make
liberal use of blackjacks to keep outsiders away. There is, of course, a
chance the attack will fail, mostly depending on the quality of the
blackjack being used. Although blackjacks are weapon-like, they are
totally useless in a melee, so blackjacks are considered [rogue
tools](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogue_Tools "wikilink"), and not
[weapon](/wiki/Category%3A_Weapons "wikilink") items. Since blackjacking
knocks targets out so fast, the [rogue](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogues "wikilink")
can generally escape [legal troubles](/wiki/Outlaw_Flag "wikilink"), unless
another [guard](/wiki/Townguard_Mobs "wikilink") or
[citizen](/wiki/Citizen_Mobs "wikilink") catches him in the act. Blackjack is
affected by your [race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink")'s innate
stealthiness. Large or clumsy [races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") may
be spotted moving in for the kill.*

Prerequisite(s): [Rogue Lore](/wiki/Rogue_Lore "wikilink").

Syntax: blackjack &lt;[mob](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink")&gt;.
