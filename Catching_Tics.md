Catching tics is an effective way for grouped spellcasters like
[mages](/wiki/Category%3A_Mages "wikilink") and
[clerics](/wiki/Category%3A_Clerics "wikilink") to somewhat alleviate
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") shortages during runs--a problem that
especially afflicts low-[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
spellcasters. This technique involves
[sleeping](/wiki/Sleep_(command) "wikilink") periodically just as a fight
begins and then [waking](/wiki/Wake "wikilink") either just after a
[tic](/wiki/Tics "wikilink") has passed or just before one's group moves
(often cued by receiving [experience
points](/wiki/Experience_Points "wikilink")). By being asleep when a
[tic](/wiki/Tics "wikilink") occurs, sleeping spellcasters will regenerate a
small amount of [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") to use. This technique
may be dangerous in some [areas](/wiki/Category%3A_Areas "wikilink") like
[Shadow Keep](/wiki/Category%3A_Shadow_Keep "wikilink") where
[aggressive](/wiki/Aggressive_Mobs "wikilink")-[wimpy](Wimpy_Mobs "wikilink")
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") will attack sleeping
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink").

It is highly recommended to put the time in your prompt to help you
catch tics. They occur on average every 30 seconds. This is equivalent
to 6 rounds of combat.
