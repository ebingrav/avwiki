*Originally, there was only one caste of clerics. They were formed under
the goddess of [Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink"). With her as their guide,
clerics stretched far and wide [healing the sick and
wounded](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Heal_Characters "wikilink")
and [protecting all from
danger](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")
with their [clerical
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink"). The [prime
requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink") of Clerics is
[WISdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"). They are specialists in
[healing](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Heal_Characters "wikilink")
[hit points](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink"), creating [protective
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink"),
and many clerical [enchantments](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink"). Unlike in the
past, they can now [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") any of the
[pantheon](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink").*

**Abbreviation:** Cle.

**[Prime Requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink"):**
[Wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink").

Clerics in General
------------------

Clerics specialize in [casting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") both
[curative](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Heal_Characters "wikilink")
and
[protective](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")
spells and are the only "[core
class](/wiki/Category%3A_Core_Classes "wikilink")" that does this; because of
this, players who enjoy serving in [healing](/wiki/Healers "wikilink")
[roles](/wiki/Category%3A_Roles "wikilink") may find this
[class](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") very appealing. As many groups,
regardless of [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), will not run without
including a [healer](/wiki/Healers "wikilink"), clerics may have less trouble
than average finding groups. Also, because popular cleric
[deities](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink"), namely
[Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") and [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink"), will increase
their [worshippers](/wiki/Worship "wikilink")'
[buffing-spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")'
[armor class](/wiki/Armor_Class "wikilink"), clerics who
[worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") these
[deities](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") may find their
[buffing
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")
in great demand, as well. Although clerics may find groups more easily
than average due to their [healing
abilities](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Heal_Characters "wikilink"),
they may find soloing much more challenging than average due to their
relatively low capacity to do damage, via both
[weapons](/wiki/Category%3A_Weapons "wikilink") and
[spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Spells "wikilink").

Cleric Creation
---------------

[Characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of any
[race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") may be clerics but some
[races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") are better suited for clericry
than others. [Characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of
[races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") with relatively-high
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"), such as [centaurs](Centaurs "wikilink"),
[deep gnomes](/wiki/Deep_Gnomes "wikilink"), and [gnomes](Gnomes "wikilink"),
tend to make excellent clerics. Likewise,
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of
[races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") with relatively-low
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"), such as [gargoyles](Gargoyles "wikilink"),
[giants](/wiki/Giants "wikilink"), and [kobolds](Kobolds "wikilink"), tend to
be poor clerics with high rates of [spellcasting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink")
failure, although some people still choose to play mages of such
[races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") because they enjoy their novelty
or difficulty.

See also [Wiseman's Avatar race comparison
chart](http://users.tpg.com.au/wyvaud/avatar_races/races.html) and/or
[Another Avatar MUD
Site](http://gishcam.tripod.com/gaming/avatar/index.html)'s [Race-Class
Comparer](http://gishcam.tripod.com/gaming/avatar/helprccl.html) for
some excellent comparative data to aid you in choosing your new cleric's
[race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink").

Clerics at Lowmort Tier
-----------------------

### Training

As with any new [character](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink"), a new
cleric's first priority should be to [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink")
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"), either as much as is possible or enough
that it may be maximized using [level
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Level_Gear "wikilink"), so as to gain as many
[practice-points](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink") per
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") as possible.

Clerics should next [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink") both their
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), which will both give them
larger average [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") gains per
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") and allow them to become adept at
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") using fewer [practice
points](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink"), and their
[constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink"), which will increase their
average [hit-point](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink") gains per
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink").

Clerics should then consider [training](/wiki/Train "wikilink") both their
[dexterity](/wiki/Dexterity "wikilink"), which may help them avoid combat
damage, and their [strength](/wiki/Strength "wikilink"), which will increase
both their [carrying capacity](/wiki/Carrying_Capacity "wikilink") and their
melee damage, although [training](/wiki/Train "wikilink") these two
[statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") is less urgent and may
be postponed if desired.

See [Statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") for more detail
about each of these five [statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink")
and their effects.

### Practicing

After maximizing their [wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"),
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), and
[constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink"), clerics may consider
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink").

Clerics may [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") only with
[trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Trainers "wikilink"); see both
[Trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Trainers "wikilink") and, more specifically,
[Cleric Trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Trainers "wikilink") for more
information on these [trainer](/wiki/Category%3A_Trainers "wikilink")
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") and on where they may be found.

Clerics should generally keep their
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink") as high as possible (usually
three more than their maximum [trainable](/wiki/Train "wikilink") amount)
while [practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") so as to avoid
needlessly wasting [practice points](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink"); see
[Intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink") for more detail about
[skill](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")-[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
costs. [Lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink") clerics may
[practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") their
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") up to 95%.

See [Cleric Lowmort Skills and
Spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Lowmort_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") for
more detail about what
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") clerics may
[practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") at
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), at what [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink")
these [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") may be
[practiced](/wiki/Practice "wikilink"), and how useful they are.

### Worshipping

Clerics, as with all [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), may
choose to [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") a [god or
goddess](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") at
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 10 or beyond. Some
[deities](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink"), though, are better
suited for clerics to [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") than others,
particularly [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") and [Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink").
Clerics intending to become [druids](/wiki/Category%3A_Druids "wikilink") at
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 50, though, must [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink")
[Roixa](/wiki/Roixa "wikilink") in order to qualify.

**Kra versus Tul-Sith:** [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") and
[Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") are most popular among Avatar MUD's
clerics. Both of these
[deities](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") will benefit their
[worshippers](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") by improving both their
[buffing-spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")'
[armor-class](/wiki/Armor_Class "wikilink") and
[healing-spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Heal_Characters "wikilink")'
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink")-costs, although [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink")
does the former more than [Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") wheras
[Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") does the latter more than
[Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink"). [Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") also both
decreases melee damage and increases regeneration of both
[mana-points](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") and
[hit-points](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink"), while [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") does
the reverse. Both of these
[deities](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") are relatively
detrimental for [enchanting](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink")
[gear](Category%3A_Gear "wikilink"). [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") also reduces
[practice-point](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink") gains per
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") at [lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") (and possibly also at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")), whereas
[Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") reduces
[experience-point](/wiki/Experience_Points "wikilink") gains, which may be an
important consideration for
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") with high [racial
tnl](Racial_TNL "wikilink"). In summary, both [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") and
[Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") help clerics enhance what they already
do best, though [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") is a bit better for
[buffing](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")
and [Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") is a bit better for
[healing](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Heal_Characters "wikilink").

**Shizaga:** [Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") is an uncommon choice for
clerics but, for some, may still be worth considering. Although
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") normally increases her
[worshippers](/wiki/Worship "wikilink")' healing-spells'
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink")-costs, she decreases these costs for
clerics, though not as much as either [Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") or
[Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") does, and she also increases
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") rengeration slightly. Clerics wishing to
[enchant](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink") [gear](Category%3A_Gear "wikilink")
would find her most beneficial to [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink").

**Roixa:** [Roixa](/wiki/Roixa "wikilink") is not generally beneficial to
[spellcasters](/wiki/Cast "wikilink"), including clerics, and greatly reduces
her [worshippers](/wiki/Worship "wikilink")'
[buffing-spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")'
[armor-class](/wiki/Armor_Class "wikilink"), though she will improve their
(non-magical) combat damage. Clerics who intend to become
[druids](/wiki/Category%3A_Druids "wikilink") must
[worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") [Roixa](/wiki/Roixa "wikilink") but would
probably find it advantageous to delay doing so for as long as possible.
Clerics who do not intend to become
[druids](/wiki/Category%3A_Druids "wikilink") would do better to
[worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") someone else.

See [Worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") for more detail regarding [worship
options](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") and their effects.

### Fighting

As with [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of other
[classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), most
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink") clerics should have little
trouble soloing from [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 1 to
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 11 very quickly. Afterward, gaining
[experience](/wiki/Experience_Points "wikilink") alone will grow increasingly
difficult and clerics should seriously consider grouping with other
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink").

**Soloing:** Clerics are not well-suited for soloing and will likely
find this terribly challenging.

**Tanking:** Clerics hardly ever serve as tanks at
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), although there are rare
exceptions. Clerics become much better able to [tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink")
groups during [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") after
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") certain [hero-tier
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") (see
below).

**Hitting:** Clerics have relatively poor capacity to cause damage,
either with [weapons](/wiki/Category%3A_Weapons "wikilink") or with
[spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Spells "wikilink"), and thus almost never serve as
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") at any
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink").

**Healing:** Clerics usually serve as [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") in
groups. Cleric [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") should normally
[wear](/wiki/Wear "wikilink") [mana gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink") to
maximize their available [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") for healing.
Clerics who run low on [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") during runs may
attempt to alleviate this problem somewhat by "[catching
tics](/wiki/Catching_Tics "wikilink")." Cleric [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink")
may also reduce their need for [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") by
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [Arcane
Knowledge](/wiki/Arcane_Knowledge "wikilink") and then using healing
[brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink") during runs. Cleric
[healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink"), when using their
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") rather than their
[brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink"), typically heal with
[Cure Light](/wiki/Cure_Light "wikilink") by default, because it is most
cost-effective, unless they are having trouble "keeping up" with damage
being done, in which case they may heal with a more powerful curative
spell like [Cure Critical](/wiki/Cure_Critical "wikilink") temporarily. Many
cleric [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") configure and use their
[prompt](/wiki/Prompt "wikilink") to [monitor](Monitor "wikilink") their
[tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink")'s [hit-points](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink") more
easily. Along with healing, many [tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") expect their
cleric [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") to re-[cast](/wiki/Cast "wikilink")
[Sanctuary](/wiki/Sanctuary "wikilink") on themselves (if not on everyone) and
[Frenzy](Frenzy "wikilink") on their [hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink")
during runs, as these two spells have relatively short durations.
Clerics may find [white marble crosses](/wiki/White_Marble_Cross "wikilink")
especially handy for renewing [Sanctuary](/wiki/Sanctuary "wikilink") on group
members during runs. When not preoccupied with healing their
[tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") or, as needed, other group members, cleric
[healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") may increase their contributions to combat
damage by using their [kill](/wiki/Kill "wikilink")
[command](/wiki/Category%3A_Commands "wikilink") to immediately target
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") that their [tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink")
has just begun to fight. In addition, cleric
[healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") who find themselves with both more time
and more [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") than they need to fulfil their
[role](/wiki/Category%3A_Roles "wikilink") as [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink")
may choose to use this extra time and [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") to
[cast](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") [offensive
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Harm_Mobs "wikilink") like
[Cause Light](/wiki/Cause_Light "wikilink") and [Harm](Harm "wikilink") and
[Earthquake](/wiki/Earthquake "wikilink") during combat.

Cleric Prestige Options
-----------------------

[Level](/wiki/Level "wikilink")-50 clerics may choose to become
[druids](/wiki/Category%3A_Druids "wikilink") instead. As
[druids](/wiki/Category%3A_Druids "wikilink"), they will
[wield](/wiki/Wield "wikilink") [missile
weapons](/wiki/Category%3A_Missile_Weapons "wikilink") like
[archers](/wiki/Category%3A_Archers "wikilink") rather than [melee
weapons](/wiki/Category%3A_Melee_Weapons "wikilink"). See
[Druids](/wiki/Category%3A_Druids "wikilink") for more information about this
[prestige class](/wiki/Category%3A_Prestige_Classes "wikilink").

Clerics at Hero Tier
--------------------

### Training

Clerics at [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") will be able to
[train](/wiki/Train "wikilink") their [wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink") five higher
than at [lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") and to [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink")
their other [statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") two higher
than at [lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"). They will continue to be able to
raise each of their [statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") up
to three more than they can [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink") them to
(respectively), unless they [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to
a [deity](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink"), in which case
these amounts may be modified slightly. See *Devoting* below for more
information about this subject.

### Practicing

Clerics should continue to [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") with [cleric
trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Trainers "wikilink") and should usually
keep their [intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink") as high as possible
while doing so, just as at [lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink").
[Hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") clerics may continue to
[practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [their
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") up to 95%.
See [Cleric Hero Skills and
Spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") for more
detail about what [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")
clerics may [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), at what [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink")
these [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") may be
[practiced](/wiki/Practice "wikilink"), and how useful they are.

### Devoting

Clerics, as with all [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), may
choose to [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to whichever
[deity](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") they have chosen to
[worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") (if any) at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") [sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 1 or
beyond.

**Kra:** Clerics who [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to
[Kra](/wiki/Kra "wikilink") will have increased maximum
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"), [constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink"),
and [strength](/wiki/Strength "wikilink"), but decerased maximum
[dexterity](/wiki/Dexterity "wikilink").

**Tul-Sith:** Clerics who [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to
[Tul-Sith](/wiki/Tul-Sith "wikilink") will have increased maximum
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink") and [constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink"),
but decreased maximum [strength](/wiki/Strength "wikilink") and
[dexterity](/wiki/Dexterity "wikilink").

See [Devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") for more detail about
[devotion](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") and its effects.

### Fighting

Clerics at [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), as at
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), continue to be well-suited and
oft-wanted to serve as group [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink"). Some
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") clerics may wish to increase their
versatility by learning to [tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") groups, as well,
which becomes easier for clerics to do at this
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink").

**Soloing:** Clerics are not well-suited for soloing and will likely
find this terribly challenging.

**Tanking:** Cleric [tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")
are not common but do exist. In fact, after
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") certain [hero-tier
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Cleric_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") such as
[dodge](/wiki/Dodge "wikilink"), [parry](Parry "wikilink"), and [shield
block](/wiki/Shield_Block "wikilink"), clerics can become highly effective
[tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink"), wearing full [tank
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") for protection and perhaps using
their [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") to heal both themselves and their
group members.

**Hitting:** Clerics have relatively poor capacity to cause damage,
either with [weapons](/wiki/Category%3A_Weapons "wikilink") or with
[spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Spells "wikilink"), and thus almost never serve as
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") at any
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink").

**Healing:** Clerics usually serve as [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") in
groups. Many, though hardly all, [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") expect their
low-[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") cleric
[healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") to carry both [mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink") and [hit
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink") during runs, to wear their [mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink") until they reach their base
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink"), and to change into their [hit
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink") afterward.
High-[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") clerics with greater base
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") may opt to wear only [hit
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink"). Clerics who run low on
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") during runs may attempt to alleviate this
problem somewhat by "[catching tics](/wiki/Catching_Tics "wikilink")" or by
using [brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink");
[brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink") may also be handy when
multiple group members need healing simultaneously. Cleric
[healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink"), when using their
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") rather than their
[brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink"), typically heal with
either [Cure Light](/wiki/Cure_Light "wikilink") or, as needed,
[Divinity](/wiki/Divinity "wikilink"). When able to do so without it
interfering with their [healing](/wiki/Healers "wikilink")
[role](/wiki/Category%3A_Roles "wikilink"), cleric
[healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") may also contribute to their group's
combat damage by [bash](/wiki/Bash "wikilink")-targeting
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") or even by
[casting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") [offensive
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Harm_Mobs "wikilink") during
combat. Even when not [tanking](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink"), cleric
[healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink") may still wish to carry a set of [tank
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") with them that they can quickly
change into during moments of mortal peril.

Clerics at Lord Tier
--------------------

Much information about [lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") is kept secret;
[lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink")-[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") clerics who wish to
learn more about their [class](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") at this
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") may (or may not) find it on the
[LordWiki site](http://lord.melanarchy.info).

Cleric Remort Options
---------------------

Clerics at [lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")
may [remort](/wiki/Remort "wikilink") into either
[priests](/wiki/Category%3A_Priests "wikilink") (either
[human](/wiki/Humans "wikilink") ones at [sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 100
or ones of their current [race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") at
[sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 200) or
[paladins](/wiki/Category%3A_Paladins "wikilink") (either
[human](/wiki/Humans "wikilink") ones at [sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 125
or ones of their current [race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") at
[sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 250). See
[Paladins](/wiki/Category%3A_Paladins "wikilink") and
[Priests](/wiki/Category%3A_Priests "wikilink") for more information about
these [remort classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Remort_Classes "wikilink").

Clerics at [lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")
may also [remort](/wiki/Remort "wikilink") into clerics of [remort
races](/wiki/Category%3A_Remort_Races "wikilink"). [Remort
races](/wiki/Category%3A_Remort_Races "wikilink") that are well-suited for
clericry include [high elves](/wiki/High_Elves "wikilink") (available *only*
to [elven](/wiki/Elves "wikilink") and [drow](Drow "wikilink") clerics at
[sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 25) and [tuataurs](Tuataurs "wikilink")
(available *only* to [lizardmen](/wiki/Lizardmen "wikilink") and
[troglodyte](/wiki/Troglodytes "wikilink") clerics at
[sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 25). Despite their relatively-high
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"), [griffons](Griffons "wikilink") are
ill-suited for [spellcasting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") and thus tend to be poor
clerics.
