*While Druids outwardly follow [Roixa](/wiki/Roixa "wikilink"), their Order
truly venerates a far more ancient Goddess, whom they credit with the
creation of the natural world. Druids have a variety of healing and
protective spells, common to [clerics](/wiki/Category%3A_Clerics "wikilink"),
but also learn much of the way of the wild, including
[bow](/wiki/Category%3A_Bows "wikilink") use, and several archery skills. They
also possess the unique ability to craft and use [mystic
arrows](/wiki/Category%3A_Mystic_Warheads "wikilink"). Like all [Prestige
Classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Prestige_Classes "wikilink"), they retain the
original class's [Prime Requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink"), in this
case, [Wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"). Druids sometimes hang up their bows
and become [Priests](/wiki/Category%3A_Priests "wikilink"), but this requires
a higher level than it would for a
[cleric](/wiki/Category%3A_Clerics "wikilink"). Because they eschew melee
weapons, Druids cannot become
[Paladins](/wiki/Category%3A_Paladins "wikilink"). Druid masters tend to favor
serene glades for their havens.*

**Abbreviation:** Dru.

**[Base Class](/wiki/Category%3A_Core_Classes "wikilink"):**
[Cleric](/wiki/Category%3A_Clerics "wikilink").

**[Prime Requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink"):**
[Wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink").
