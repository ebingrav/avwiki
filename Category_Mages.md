*The mysterious [arcane knowledge](/wiki/Arcane_Knowledge "wikilink") that
these persons wield is often frightening. At the outset, these people
may not seem to present a challenge for a good sword or a stout club,
but as their studies grow, they have been found to be formidable
opponents. They can be limited by their power level, but more than one
mage has been known to inflict great fear in others and when they were
seemingly defeated, disappear. Not dead of course, but preparing to
return and finish the job they started. The [prime
requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink") of Mages is
[INTelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), allowing them to
[learn](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") powerful
[spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Spells "wikilink").*

**Abbreviation:** Mag.

**[Prime Requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink"):**
[Intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink").

Mages in General
----------------

Mages specialize in casting [offensive
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Harm_Mobs "wikilink"). They
can generally do more damage than
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of many other
[classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") as long as they do not become
depleted of their [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink"); this dependence upon
their [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") supply may be their greatest
weakness. Mages, with their in-class transportation spells and [surging
ability](/wiki/Surge "wikilink") and such, can obtain and
[enchant](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink") [gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink")
more easily than [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of most
other [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), which makes them rather
handy either for equipping their alternate
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") or for earning money by
selling [enchanted](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink")
[gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink") to other players. Their ability to
solo more easily than [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of
most other [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") may make mages
especially appealing to players whose circumstances do not always allow
them to conveniently fight in groups. Mages, sadly, tend to die more
frequently over time than
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of other
[classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") for a variety of reasons,
including their having lower [hit-points](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink") and
their tending to [wear](/wiki/Wear "wikilink") less-protective
[gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink") during combat, particularly [mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink"). For this reason, it can be
argued that [tank gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") is more
important to mages than any other set of
[gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink"), accompanied by an ability to change
into it rapidly (using effective [gear-changing
aliases](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear_Changing_Aliases "wikilink")) at moments of
mortal danger.

Mage Creation
-------------

[Characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of any
[race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") may be mages but some
[races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") are far better suited for magery
than others. [Characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of
[races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") with relatively-high
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), such as
[centaurs](/wiki/Centaurs "wikilink"), [draconians](Draconians "wikilink"),
[drow](/wiki/Drow "wikilink"), and [elves](Elves "wikilink"), tend to make
excellent mages. Likewise,
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of
[races](/wiki/Category%3A_races "wikilink") with relatively-low
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), such as
[gargoyles](/wiki/Gargoyles "wikilink"), [giants](Giants "wikilink"),
[goblins](/wiki/Goblins "wikilink"), [troglodytes](Troglodytes "wikilink"),
and [verbits](/wiki/Verbits "wikilink"), tend to be poor mages with high rates
of [spellcasting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") failure, although some people still
choose to play mages of such [races](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink")
because they enjoy their novelty or difficulty.

See also [Wiseman's Avatar race comparison
chart](http://users.tpg.com.au/wyvaud/avatar_races/races.html) and/or
[Another Avatar MUD
Site](http://gishcam.tripod.com/gaming/avatar/index.html)'s [Race-Class
Comparer](http://gishcam.tripod.com/gaming/avatar/helprccl.html) for
some excellent comparative data to aid you in choosing your new mage's
[race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink").

Mages at Lowmort Tier
---------------------

### Training

As with any new [character](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink"), a new
mage's first priority should be to [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink")
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"), either as much as is possible or enough
that it may be maximized using [level
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Level_Gear "wikilink"), so as to gain as many
[practice-points](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink") per
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") as possible.

Mages should next [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink") both their
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), which will (1) give them larger
average [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") gains and (2) increase their
spell damage and (3) allow them to become adept at
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") using fewer [practice
points](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink"), and their
[constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink"), which will increase their
average [hit-point](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink") gains per
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink").

Mages should then consider [training](/wiki/Train "wikilink") both their
[dexterity](/wiki/Dexterity "wikilink"), which may help them avoid combat
damage, and their [strength](/wiki/Strength "wikilink"), which will increase
both their [carrying capacity](/wiki/Carrying_Capacity "wikilink") and their
melee damage, although [training](/wiki/Train "wikilink") these two
[statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") is less urgent and may
be postponed if desired.

See [Statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") for more detail
about each of these five [statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink")
and their effects.

### Practicing

After maximizing their [wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"),
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), and
[constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink"), mages may consider
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink").

Mages may [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") only with
[trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Trainers "wikilink"); see both
[Trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Trainers "wikilink") and, more specifically,
[Mage Trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Trainers "wikilink") for more
information on these [trainer](/wiki/Category%3A_Trainers "wikilink")
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") and on where they may be found.

Mages should generally keep their
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink") as high as possible (usually
three more than their maximum [trainable](/wiki/Train "wikilink") amount)
while [practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") so as to avoid
needlessly wasting [practice points](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink"); see
[Intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink") for more detail about
[skill](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")-[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
costs. [Lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink") mages may
[practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [their
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Lowmort_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") up to
95%.

See [Mage Lowmort Skills and
Spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Lowmort_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") for more
detail about what [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")
mages may [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") at
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), at what [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink")
these [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") may be
[practiced](/wiki/Practice "wikilink"), and how useful they are.

### Worshipping

Mages, as with all [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), may choose
to [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") a [god or
goddess](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") at
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 10 or beyond. Some
[deities](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink"), though, are better
suited for mages to [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") than others,
particularly [Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink"), [Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink"),
and [Quixoltan](/wiki/Quixoltan "wikilink").

**Bhyss versus Shizaga:** [Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink") and
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") are most popular among Avatar MUD's mages.
Both of these [deities](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink")
decrease their worshippers' melee damage but increase their worshippers'
spell damage, which is very beneficial to mages because they do more
damage via their [spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Spells "wikilink") than via their
[weapons](Category%3A_Weapons "wikilink"); [Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink"),
however, does this more than [Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") does, making
him ideal for mages wishing to maximize their overall combat damage.
[Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink"), however, also reduces gains in both
[hit-points](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink") and
[practice-points](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink") over time, making his
worshippers easier to kill and slightly slower to develop
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink"); for these reasons,
mages wanting to [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") [Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink")
may defer doing so until either [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") or
[lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"). And
[Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink") also hurts with
[enchanting](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink"), whereas
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") helps with it, making
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") ideal for mages wishing to maximize their
effectiveness at [enchanting](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink")
[gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink"). Both [Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink") and
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") decrease their worshippers'
[buffing-spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")'
[armor class](/wiki/Armor_Class "wikilink"), making their worshippers less
desired by other [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink"),
especially [tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink"), to provide them with [such
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink").
In summary, [Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink") may be better suited for mages who
plan to do nothing other than [hit](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") within groups,
whereas [Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") may be better suited for mages who
expect to solo some, particularly to obtain and
[enchant](/wiki/Enchanting "wikilink")
[gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink")--tasks for which mages are
well-suited.

See [Worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") for more detail regarding [worship
options](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") and their effects.

### Fighting

As with [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of other
[classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), most
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink") mages should have little
trouble soloing from [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 1 to
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 11 very quickly. Afterward, gaining
[experience](/wiki/Experience_Points "wikilink") alone will grow increasingly
difficult and mages should seriously consider grouping with other
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink"). Once mages
[practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [surge](/wiki/Surge "wikilink") at
[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 25, they will find soloing somewhat easier
once again.

**Soloing:** Mages may find soloing easier than
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of most other
[classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") once they have
[practiced](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [surge](/wiki/Surge "wikilink"). Some find
mage soloing to be rather tedious, overly repetitive, and/or lonesome,
and may do so only if groups are not avaialable. Because mage soloing
requires long periods of [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") regeneration
between kills, mage soloers may opt to engage in additional real-life
activities while their [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink")
are [sleeping](/wiki/Sleep_(command) "wikilink").

**Tanking:** Mages hardly ever serve as [tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") at
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), although there are rare
exceptions. Mages become much better able to [tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink")
groups during [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") after
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") certain [hero-tier
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") (see below).

**Hitting:** Mages usually serve as [hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") in
[lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink") groups. Mage
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") should normally [wear](/wiki/Wear "wikilink")
[mana gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink") to maximize their
available [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink"), upon which they are highly
dependent for doing damage. Mages who run low on
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") during runs may attempt to alleviate this
problem somewhat by "[catching tics](/wiki/Catching_Tics "wikilink")." Mage
hitters typically fight by waiting for their [tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") to
start fighting a [mob](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink"), immediately using
their [kill](/wiki/Kill "wikilink") [command](Category%3A_Commands "wikilink")
targetted at this same [mob](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink"), and then
[casting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") a single-target [attack
spell](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Harm_Mobs "wikilink") (usually
via an [alias](/wiki/Alias "wikilink")) repeatedly without specifying any
target until this [mob](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") is dead. Whenever
their [tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") is fighting more than a few
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") simultaneously, mage
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") may consider [casting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") an
area-effect [attack
spell](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Harm_Mobs "wikilink") instead
of a single-target [attack
spell](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Harm_Mobs "wikilink"). Mage
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") should [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[chaos channel](/wiki/Chaos_Channel "wikilink") as soon as possible, as it
will rougly halve their [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") costs for all of
their [attack
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Harm_Mobs "wikilink") while
they are grouped.

**Healing:** Although [clerics](/wiki/Category%3A_Clerics "wikilink") and
their like are usually preferred as [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink"), they
are not always available. [Lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
mages may substitute for [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink")
of such [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") by
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [arcane
knowledge](/wiki/Arcane_Knowledge "wikilink"), by collecting certain
[brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink") (such as [shards of
durrite](/wiki/Shard_Of_Durrite "wikilink"), [limestone
staves](/wiki/Limestone_Staff "wikilink"), [fingerbones of
Senex](/wiki/Fingerbone_Of_Senex "wikilink"), [gold dragon
orbs](/wiki/Gold_Dragon_Orb "wikilink"), and [white marble
crosses](/wiki/White_Marble_Cross "wikilink")) and by both
[holding](/wiki/Hold "wikilink") and [brandishing](Brandish "wikilink") these
as needed during runs.

Mage Prestige Options
---------------------

[Level](/wiki/Level "wikilink")-50 mages may choose to become
[wizards](/wiki/Category%3A_Wizards "wikilink") instead. As
[wizards](/wiki/Category%3A_Wizards "wikilink"), they will gain less [hit
points](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink") but more [mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink"),
they will do less melee damage but more spell damage, and they will
forfeit their ability to learn certain [psionic
spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Psionicist_Lowmort_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")
during [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") but will gain other [unique
abilities](/wiki/Category%3A_Wizard_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")
instead. See [Wizards](/wiki/Category%3A_Wizards "wikilink") for more
information about this [prestige
class](/wiki/Category%3A_Prestige_Classes "wikilink").

Mages at Hero Tier
------------------

### Training

Mages at [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") will be able to
[train](/wiki/Train "wikilink") their [intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink")
five higher than at [lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") and to [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink")
their other [statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") two higher
than at [lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"). They will continue to be able to
raise each of their [statistics](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") up
to three more than they can [train](/wiki/Train "wikilink") them to
(respectively), unless they [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to
a [deity](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink"), in which case
these amounts may be modified slightly. See *Devoting* below for more
information about this subject.

### Practicing

Mages should continue to [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink")
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") with [mage
trainers](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Trainers "wikilink") and should usually keep
their [intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink") as high as possible while
doing so, just as at [lowmort](/wiki/Category%3A_Lowmort "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink").
[Hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") mages may continue to
[practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [their
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") up to 95%.
See [Mage Hero Skills and
Spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") for more
detail about what [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")
mages may [practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"), at what [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink")
these [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") may be
[practiced](/wiki/Practice "wikilink"), and how useful they are.

### Devoting

Mages, as with all [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), may choose
to [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to whichever
[deity](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink") they have chosen to
[worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") (if any) at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") [sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 1 or
beyond.

**Bhyss:** Mages who [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to
[Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink") will experience both benefits and detriments;
on one hand, they will have one greater maximum
[intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"), which may or may not enable
them to become adept at
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") more quickly and
which will increase their spell damage slightly; however, on the other
hand, they will also have both one less maxium
[constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink") and one less maximum
[dexterity](/wiki/Dexterity "wikilink"), which will reduce both their
[hit-point](/wiki/Hit_Points "wikilink") gains and their ability to avoid
damage and will thus make them a bit more "splatty" than they already
are. In summary, if and when [Bhyss](/wiki/Bhyss "wikilink") worshippers
[devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves, they will dish out damage even
better but take it even worse.

**Shizaga:** Mages who [devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") will have one less maximum
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink"), which may--or may not--reduce their
[practice-point](/wiki/Practice_Points "wikilink") gains at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"); thus, mages worshipping
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink") who are eager to learn all of [their hero
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") as quickly
as possible may wish to postpone [devotion](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") until
later in [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"). If and when they
[devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") themselves to
[Shizaga](/wiki/Shizaga "wikilink"), they may benefit slightly from having one
more maximum [dexterity](/wiki/Dexterity "wikilink") if and when they are
attacked.

See [Devote](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") for more detail about
[devotion](/wiki/Devotion "wikilink") and its effects.

### Fighting

Mages at [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") may have more trouble finding
groups than [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of many
other [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"). This is especially true
at lower [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") sublevels, where mages tend
to die more readily and tend to run short of
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") during runs.

**Soloing:** Mages who solo may find it best to regenerate in [mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink"), then to change into either
[tank gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") or [hit
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink") in order to fight, using a
[surged](/wiki/Surge "wikilink") [disintegrate spell](/wiki/Disintegrate "wikilink")
to kill [mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink"). Popular
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") for
low-[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") mages to [kill](Kill "wikilink")
solo for experience include [Shashwat
Nisha](/wiki/Category%3A_Shashwat_Nisha "wikilink")'s "yaks" and cobblynau,
[Sir Michael's
Stronghold](/wiki/Category%3A_Sir_Michael's_Stronghold "wikilink")'s wandering
peasants and such, and [Shadow
Keep](/wiki/Category%3A_Shadow_Keep "wikilink")'s rotting corpses and hound
dogs.

**Tanking:** Mages [tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")
are not common but do exist. In fact, after
[practicing](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") certain [hero-tier
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Mage_Hero_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink"),
particularly [dodge](/wiki/Dodge "wikilink"), [parry](Parry "wikilink"),
[shield block](/wiki/Shield_Block "wikilink"), and [charge
shield](/wiki/Charge_Shield "wikilink"), mages can become formidable
[tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink"), wearing full [tank
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") for protection and using
[disintegrate](/wiki/Disintegrate "wikilink"), [acid
rain](/wiki/Acid_Rain "wikilink"), and [charged
shields](/wiki/Charge_Shield "wikilink") to deal damage.

**Hitting:** Mages usually serve as [hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") in
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") groups. Many, though hardly all,
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") [tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") expect
their low-[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") mage
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") to carry both [mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink") and [hit
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink") during runs, to wear their [mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink") until they reach their base
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink"), and to change into their [hit
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink") afterward.
High-[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") mages with greater base
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") may opt to wear only [hit
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink"). Mages who run low on
[mana](/wiki/Mana_Points "wikilink") during runs may attempt to alleviate this
problem somewhat by "[catching tics](/wiki/Catching_Tics "wikilink")." Mage
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") typically fight by waiting for their
[tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") to start fighting a
[mob](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink"), immediately using their
[kill](/wiki/Kill "wikilink") [command](Category%3A_Commands "wikilink")
targetted at this same [mob](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink"), and then
[casting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") [disintegrate](/wiki/Disintegrate "wikilink")
(usually via an [alias](/wiki/Alias "wikilink")) repeatedly without specifying
any target until this [mob](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") is dead.
Whenever their [tank](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") is fighting more than a few
[mobs](/wiki/Category%3A_Mobs "wikilink") simultaneously, mage
[hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") may consider [casting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink")
[acid rain](/wiki/Acid_Rain "wikilink") instead of
[disintegrate](/wiki/Disintegrate "wikilink"). Even when not
[tanking](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink"), mage [hitters](/wiki/Hitters "wikilink") may
still wish to carry a set of [tank
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") with them that they can quickly
change into in emergencies.

**Healing:** Mages occasionally serve as [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink")
in [hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") groups, possibly when no
[clerics](/wiki/Category%3A_Clerics "wikilink"),
[druids](/wiki/Category%3A_Druids "wikilink"), or
[priests](/wiki/Category%3A_Priests "wikilink") are available. A few
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") [tanks](/wiki/Tanks "wikilink") may even
prefer to group mages who heal with
[brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink") over
[clerics](/wiki/Category%3A_Clerics "wikilink") who heal with
[spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Spells "wikilink"). Mages who maintain a stockpile
of healing [brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink"), such as
[gold dragon orbs](/wiki/Gold_Dragon_Orb "wikilink"), [emerald sceptres of
light](/wiki/Emerald_Sceptre_Of_Light "wikilink"), [black staffs of
Typhus](/wiki/Black_Staff_Of_Typhus "wikilink"), and [white marble
crosses](/wiki/White_Marble_Cross "wikilink"), may thus be able to find groups
slightly more often than mages who do not.
[Brandishes](/wiki/Category%3A_Brandishes "wikilink") are best collected well
before they are needed, as they tend to be consumed on runs faster than
they can be easily replenished. Mage [healers](/wiki/Healers "wikilink")
usually wear [hit gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink") for runs,
though [mana gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink") may sometimes be
useful or even [tank gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") in
emergencies.

Mages at Lord Tier
------------------

Mages tend to find groups more easily at [lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") than at
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink")
[tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink"). They may also
[practice](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") a
[skill](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") that enables them to
rename [objects](/wiki/Category%3A_Objects "wikilink"). Much information about
[lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") is kept
secret; [lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink")-[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") mages who wish
to learn more about their [class](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") at
this [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink") may (or may not) find it on
the [LordWiki site](http://lord.melanarchy.info).

Mage Remort Options
-------------------

Mages at [lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")
may [remort](/wiki/Remort "wikilink") into
[sorcerers](/wiki/Category%3A_Sorcerers "wikilink"), either
[human](/wiki/Humans "wikilink") ones at [sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 100
or ones of their current [race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") at
[sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 200. See
[Sorcerers](/wiki/Category%3A_Sorcerers "wikilink") for more information about
this [remort class](/wiki/Category%3A_Remort_Classes "wikilink").

Mages at [lord](/wiki/Lord "wikilink") [tier](/wiki/Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")
may also [remort](/wiki/Remort "wikilink") into mages of [remort
races](/wiki/Category%3A_Remort_Races "wikilink"). [Remort
races](/wiki/Category%3A_Remort_Races "wikilink") that are well-suited for
magery include [dragons](/wiki/Dragons "wikilink") (available to all mages at
[sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 100), [high
elves](/wiki/High_Elves "wikilink") (available *only* to
[elven](/wiki/Elves "wikilink") and [drow](Drow "wikilink") mages at
[sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 25), and [sprites](Sprites "wikilink")
(available to all mages at [sublevel](/wiki/Sublevel "wikilink") 25). Despite
their relatively-high [intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"),
[griffons](/wiki/Griffons "wikilink") are ill-suited for
[spellcasting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink") and thus tend to be poor mages.
