*The rogue is not concerned with the "virtues" of knighthood, nobility,
or morality. The rogue serves no one but themselves. Rogues are masters
of deception, [theft](/wiki/Steal "wikilink"), and the effective but honorless
ability to stab others in the back. These fellows are not disciplined
like soldiers, but they tend to be very quick, often eluding more
powerful foes and are quick to relieve the general populace of their
heavy coins and possessions. These untrustworthy souls utilize their
knowledge of the city to evaluate and [steal](/wiki/Steal "wikilink") items of
great value, [fence](/wiki/Fence "wikilink") them at great profit,
[surprise](/wiki/Sneak_Attack "wikilink") their enemies or 'marks,' and evade
their antagonists with great alacrity. The [prime
requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink") of rogues is
[DEXterity](/wiki/Dexterity "wikilink") which makes them very slippery
opponents. [Strength](/wiki/Strength "wikilink") is desirable to make their
primary attack, the [backstab](/wiki/Backstab "wikilink"), more powerful and
to allow them to carry more loot.
[Intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink") is a big plus, allowing them to
learn their [skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogue_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")
quickly and expertly as well as to assist their streetsmarts. And as
with all [classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), a hardy
[constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink") will help them survive in the
lethal dark alleyways and sidestreets of the big cities.*

**Abbreviation:** Rog.

So, you've decided to create a rogue... Now what?

Although characters of any [race](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") may
become rogues, some races are better suited for roguishness than others,
such as [centaurs](/wiki/Centaurs "wikilink"), [kzinti](Kzinti "wikilink"),
and [orcs](/wiki/Orcs "wikilink"), partly due to their high

### Worshipping

Hmmm... Whom to worship? And when?

Rogues, like all classes, may choose to [worship](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") at
level 10. Although rogues may worship any
[god](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink"), some dieties are more
suitable for rogues than others. [Tor](/wiki/Tor "wikilink") is by far the
most popular god for rogues to worship; because Tor can reduce practice
gains, though, some rogues prefer to postpone worshipping him until
[hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") [tier](Category%3A_Tiers "wikilink")
after sublevel 101.
