*In the words of Thrull, "Wizards are what all
[Mages](/wiki/Category%3A_Mages "wikilink") should have been." Wizards are
mystically stronger than [Mages](/wiki/Category%3A_Mages "wikilink"), but
physically weaker. They get more mana and their spells are more
effective, but they also get lower hp gains and are, at best,
unimpressive in melee combat. They can also learn some very rare spells
by researching them from obscure
[spellbooks](/wiki/Category%3A_Spellbooks "wikilink"). These spells are not
listed on their [skill
lists](/wiki/Category%3A_Wizard_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink"). Like all
[Prestige Classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Prestige_Classes "wikilink"), they retain
the original class's [Prime Requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink"), in
this case, [Intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink"). Wizards cannot
become [Sorcerors](/wiki/Category%3A_Sorcerers "wikilink"). They disdain what
they see as the [Sorceror](/wiki/Category%3A_Sorcerers "wikilink")'s
crutch-like dependence on the Dark Powers. Wizard trainers can sometimes
be found researching lost tomes in musty libraries.*

**Abbreviation:** Wzd.

**[Base Class](/wiki/Category%3A_Core_Classes "wikilink"):**
[Mage](/wiki/Category%3A_Mages "wikilink").

**[Prime Requisite](/wiki/Prime_Requisite "wikilink"):**
[Intelligence](/wiki/Intelligence "wikilink").

Wizard Creation
---------------

Note: The wizard trainer mentions rather clearly that wisdom is an
important factor of becoming a wizard. Half-Elves cannot become wizards
with 18 max wisdom, so you will presumably need at least 19
[wisdom](wisdom "wikilink") to become a wizard. Races presumed to have
enough wisdom: [Gnomes](/wiki/Gnome "wikilink"), [Deep
Gnomes](/wiki/Deep_Gnome "wikilink"), [Centaurs](Centaur "wikilink"),
[Tuataurs](/wiki/Tuataur "wikilink"), and [High Elves](High_Elf "wikilink").
