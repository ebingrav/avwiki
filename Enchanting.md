Enchanting is the process of using [Enchant
Weapon](/wiki/Enchant_Weapon "wikilink"), [Enchant
Armor](/wiki/Enchant_Armor "wikilink") and [Enchant
Bow](/wiki/Enchant_Bow "wikilink") to improve an item of that type. While
enchanting gear is not necessary, gear with enchantments are highly
prized, especially hero level gear that is either single or double
[brilled](/wiki/Brilling "wikilink").

Enchanting carries a price, however. One can either successfully enchant
the item, adding either +1 [hr](/wiki/Hit_Roll "wikilink")/+1
[dr](/wiki/Damage_Roll "wikilink") (+2/+2 with a brilled enchant weapon), -2
[AC](/wiki/Armor_Class "wikilink") (-3 AC with a brilled enchant armor) or +2
hit roll (+3 with a brilled enchant bow) respectively, the item can
[fade](/wiki/Fading "wikilink"), explode, or nothing at all can happen.

Enchanting items that already have enchantments on them appears to be
more difficult, though much is governed by luck. While some items are
easier to enchant than others, gear with armor class modifiers on them
already, such as [tank gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero_Tank_Gear "wikilink")
tends to be harder to enchant than items without, such as [ mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero_Mana_Gear "wikilink").

One can improve the odds of successfully enchanting by
[worshipping](/wiki/Worship "wikilink") certain [Gods and
Goddesses](/wiki/Category%3A_Gods_And_Goddesses "wikilink"), though results
still appear to be somewhat random.
