Fading is the process of using the spells [enchant
weapon](/wiki/Enchant_Weapon "wikilink"), [enchant
armor](/wiki/Enchant_Armor "wikilink") or [enchant
bow](/wiki/Enchant_Bow "wikilink") with the hopes of receiving the message
"<item> grows brightly, then fades." Fading removes all current
enchantments on the item, as well as any
[flags](/wiki/Category%3A_Object_Flags "wikilink"). Flags such as
[Anti-Good](/wiki/Anti-Good_Flag "wikilink"), [Magic](/wiki/Magic_Flag "wikilink"),
[Nodrop](/wiki/Nodrop_Flag "wikilink") and
[Noremove](/wiki/Noremove_Flag "wikilink") are the primary flags people
attempt to remove by fading, though the latter two are more commonly
removed by using [remove curse](/wiki/Remove_Curse "wikilink").

Since many [Hero](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero "wikilink") level gear is
[Anti-Good](/wiki/Anti-Good_Flag "wikilink") by default, fading is a practical
requirement for use (except for
[Shadowfist](/wiki/Category%3A_Shadowfists "wikilink") and
[Sorcerer](/wiki/Category%3A_Sorcerers "wikilink") characters who have
practiced [Demonic Intent](/wiki/Demonic_Intent "wikilink"), who in turn would
need items that load [Anti-Evil](/wiki/Anti-Evil_Flag "wikilink") to be faded
prior to their use.

Items with the [magic flag](/wiki/Magic_Flag "wikilink") need to be faded
prior to being [transmuted](/wiki/Transmute "wikilink"). This most commonly
happens when one wants to dual-wield the same weapon, but lacks the
rogue skills [balance](/wiki/Balance "wikilink") and
[poise](/wiki/Poise "wikilink"). With the magic flag removed, an item can be
transmuted lighter or heavier to be used in the offhand or main wield
slot, respectively.

Since fading is a random result of the enchant spells, very often an
item can be rendered unusable in the process of trying to fade it. For
instance, an item that loads [Anti-Good](/wiki/Anti-Good_Flag "wikilink") can
receive the [Anti-Evil flag](/wiki/Anti-Evil_Flag "wikilink") and the
[Anti-Neutral flag](/wiki/Anti-Neutral_Flag "wikilink") when a good aligned
caster "successfully" enchants the weapon. Since both successfully
enchanting and fading add a level to the item, an item which still
hasn't faded at level 54 (for [hero
armor](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero_Gear "wikilink")) or level 56 (for [hero
weapons](/wiki/Category%3A_Hero_Gear "wikilink")) must either be
[harmed](/wiki/Harmonizing "wikilink") or scrapped.
