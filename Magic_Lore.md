*Over the years, practitioners of the magical arts have gathered
together numerous little tidbits of knowledge about the strange ways
that [things](/wiki/Category%3A_Objects "wikilink") behave. The more useful
parts of this information are collectively known as "magic lore". Those
who have assimilated this lore know instinctively the way that an
[enchanted](/wiki/Enchant_Weapon "wikilink")
[weapon](/wiki/Category%3A_Weapons "wikilink") quivers when it is waved, the
degree of power within a [potion](/wiki/Category%3A_Potions "wikilink"), the
particular manner in which an evil doublet will refuse to lace up, and a
veritable gaggle of other bits of information that collectively make
magic lore a useful addition to one's knowledge. Additionally, those
versed in magic lore who have reached [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") 25 are
always exactly aware of what [spells and
skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink")
[affect](/wiki/Affects "wikilink") them and for how long.*

Prerequisite(s): [Lore](/wiki/Lore "wikilink").

Syntax: [examine](/wiki/Examine "wikilink")
&lt;[object](/wiki/Category%3A_Objects "wikilink")&gt;.

This [skill](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") is handy for
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of
[spellcasting](/wiki/Cast "wikilink")
[classes](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink"), and is useful for any
[character](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") wanting to check
his/her/its
[buffing-spells](/wiki/Category%3A_Skills_And_Spells_That_Buff_Characters "wikilink")'
duration.
