[Objects](/wiki/Category%3A_Objects "wikilink") of this
[type](/wiki/Category%3A_Object_Types "wikilink") are practically always
permanent fixtures of [areas](/wiki/Category%3A_Areas "wikilink") that
[characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") may not [pick
up](/wiki/Get "wikilink"). Markings are most commonly used as signposts and/or
to [open](/wiki/Open "wikilink") [doors](/wiki/Category%3A_Doors "wikilink"),
sometimes only for [characters](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") of
one specific [class](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink").

All markings have four
[type](/wiki/Category%3A_Object_Types "wikilink")-specific
[values](/wiki/Category%3A_Object_Values "wikilink"): one specifies how
high-[level](/wiki/Level "wikilink") a
[character](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") must be to see it,
another specifies other restrictions on its visibility (like only those
who have [practiced](/wiki/Practice "wikilink") [rogue
lore](/wiki/Rogue_Lore "wikilink") or only those who are
[affected](/wiki/Affects "wikilink") by [detect
hidden](/wiki/Detect_Hidden "wikilink") may see it), another specifies which
[door](/wiki/Category%3A_Doors "wikilink") in its
[room](/wiki/Category%3A_Rooms "wikilink") (if any) it affects when it is
[pushed](/wiki/Push "wikilink") or [pulled](Pull "wikilink"), and yet another
specifies exactly how this [door](/wiki/Category%3A_Doors "wikilink") will be
affected by such usage (like being [opened](/wiki/Open "wikilink") or being
[locked](/wiki/Lock "wikilink")).

One good example of markings are
[Midgaard](/wiki/Category%3A_Midgaard "wikilink")'s "thief marks," which serve
to help [rogues](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogues "wikilink") to find [their
guildmaster](/wiki/Guildmaster_(rogue) "wikilink").
