*The Prime Requisite of a [character](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink")
is the ability score which affects and modifies his capabilities most.
Every [class](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") has its own prime
requisite. The most immediate affect a prime requisite has is that it
starts one point higher than your other [ability
scores](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") do. Noting that all [ability
scores](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") start at 13, this means your
prime requisite starts at 14 (which can be further modified due to the
[racial](/wiki/Category%3A_Races "wikilink") modifiers which are immediately
applied at time of creation of your character.) The second most
immediate affect the prime requisite has is that is trainable 3 points
higher than your other [ability
scores](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink"). I.e., the prime requisite
for a [human](/wiki/Humans "wikilink") of any
[class](/wiki/Category%3A_Classes "wikilink") is trainable to 24, instead of
their normal maximum of 21. Even though your prime requisite is
important, please note that all [ability
scores](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") affect your
[character](/wiki/Category%3A_Characters "wikilink") in one way or another.
Without a high [constitution](/wiki/Constitution "wikilink") you'll find your
hit points gained per level is low, without high
[wisdom](/wiki/Wisdom "wikilink") your practices gained per level will be low,
etc, etc.*
