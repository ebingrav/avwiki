This [gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink")-changing system uses up to four
different [containers](/wiki/Category%3A_Containers "wikilink"), most often
the following four: one for a full set of [tanking
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink"), one for a full set of [hitting
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink"), one for a full set of [mana
gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink"), and one for all miscellaneous
gear. This last category of [gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink") would
include items like [leveling gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Level_Gear "wikilink"),
stealth gear, [rogue tools](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogue_Tools "wikilink"),
[fletching kits](/wiki/Fletching_Tools_And_Materials "wikilink"), [Bowie
knives](/wiki/Standard_Issue_Bowie_Knife "wikilink"),
[whetstones](/wiki/Whetstone "wikilink"), and so forth.

Benefits of using this system
-----------------------------

-   You don't need to rewrite these aliases when you get a new piece
    of gear.
-   You don't need to think about what gear you are currently wearing
    when you use any alias.
-   You don't need to worry about any items being put in the wrong bag.
-   You don't need to worry about any items being worn in the
    wrong slot.

Detriments of using this system
-------------------------------

-   You do need to etch each and every individual piece of
    gear involved.
-   You may not be able to use items that you receive that have already
    been etched.
-   You will produce lots of spam every time that you use some of
    these aliases.

Example Etchings
----------------

Here are suggested keywords to [etch](/wiki/Etch "wikilink") on both
[containers](/wiki/Category%3A_Containers "wikilink") and
[gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink") using this system:

-   *miscgearbag*: a [container](/wiki/Category%3A_Containers "wikilink") for
    miscellaneous [gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink").
    -   *levelgear*: any [gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink") used to
        maximize [stats](/wiki/Category%3A_Statistics "wikilink") when
        increasing in [level](/wiki/Level "wikilink").
    -   *stealthgear*: any [gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Gear "wikilink") used for
        stealth, such as a [Ring of the
        Rat](/wiki/Ring_Of_The_Rat "wikilink").
    -   *corpsegear*: an [item](/wiki/Category%3A_Objects "wikilink") for
        working with corpses, such as a [Bowie
        Knife](/wiki/Standard_Issue_Bowie_Knife "wikilink").

<!-- -->

-   *tankgearbag*: a [container](/wiki/Category%3A_Containers "wikilink") for
    a full set of [tanking gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink").
    -   *tankgear-l*: a tanking light.
    -   *tankgear-h0*: a tanking wielded weapon for rogues, such as a
        [Sharp Dagger Named
        'Poison'](/wiki/Sharp_Dagger_Named_'Poison' "wikilink").
    -   *tankgear-h1*: a tanking wielded weapon.
    -   *tankgear-h2*: a tanking shield.
    -   *tankgear-x*: a tanking held-item.
    -   *tankgear-c*: any item of tanking gear that is not a light,
        weapon, shield, or held-item.

<!-- -->

-   *archgearbag*: a [container](/wiki/Category%3A_Containers "wikilink") for
    a full set of [archery gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Archer_Gear "wikilink").
    -   *archgear-l*: an archery light.
    -   *archgear-hg*: a longbow for grouping
    -   *archgear-hs*: a crossbow for soloing.
    -   *archgear-c*: any item of archery gear that is not a light
        or weapon.

<!-- -->

-   *hitgearbag*: a [container](/wiki/Category%3A_Containers "wikilink") for a
    full set of [hitting gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Hit_Gear "wikilink").
    -   *hitgear-l*: a hitting light.
    -   *hitgear-h1*: a hitting wielded weapon.
    -   *hitgear-h2*: a hitting offhand weapon.
    -   *hitgear-h3*: a hitting shield for bashing, such as a [Black and
        Silver Shield](/wiki/Black_And_Silver_Shield "wikilink").
    -   *hitgear-x*: a hitting held-item.
    -   *hitgear-c*: any item of hitting gear that is not a light,
        weapon, shield, or held-item.

<!-- -->

-   *managearbag*: a [container](/wiki/Category%3A_Containers "wikilink") for
    a full set of [mana gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Mana_Gear "wikilink").
    -   *managear-l*: a mana light.
    -   *managear-h1*: a mana wielded weapon
    -   *managear-h2*: a mana offhand weapon.
    -   *managear-h3*: a mana shield for bashing, such as an [Adamantite
        Shield](/wiki/Adamantite_Shield "wikilink").
    -   *managear-x*: a mana held-item.
    -   *managear-c*: any item of mana gear that is not a light, weapon,
        shield, or held-item.

All [tanking gear](/wiki/Category%3A_Tank_Gear "wikilink") could be
[etched](/wiki/Etch "wikilink") simply as *tankgear*, and so forth, to allow
for simpler aliases; however, using these variants shown above provides
some advantages, as in the following examples: (1)
[etching](/wiki/Etch "wikilink") [lights](Category%3A_Lights "wikilink") like
this can help [psionicists](/wiki/Category%3A_Psionicists "wikilink") to
create effective [torching](/wiki/Torch_(spell) "wikilink") aliases; (2)
[etching](/wiki/Etch "wikilink") [weapons](Category%3A_Weapons "wikilink") and
shields like this can help to ensure that these items are always
equipped in their proper slots and whatnot; and (3)
[etching](/wiki/Etch "wikilink") held items like this may help
[rogues](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogues "wikilink") to create effective more aliases
for using some of their
[skills](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogue_Skills_And_Spells "wikilink") and
[tools](/wiki/Category%3A_Rogue_Tools "wikilink").
